Colonel John Spiszer USA Ret. Biography

Mr. John Spiszer graduated from the United States Military Academy in 1984 with a commission in the Infantry and retired as a Colonel in 2014. Prior to his current duties, he served as the Deputy G35 for U.S. Army Europe and Africa in Wiesbaden, Germany. Mr. Spiszer’s military education includes the Infantry Officer’s Basic and Advanced Courses, Infantry Mortar Leader’s Course, Command and General Staff Officer’s Course, the Advanced Military Studies Program (SAMS), the Naval War College (NWC), and the Joint and Combined Warfighting Course. He has advanced degrees including a Master of Science in Administration from Central Michigan University, a Master of Military Arts and Science from SAMS, and a Master of Arts in National Security and Strategic Studies from the Naval War College.

His initial Army assignments included Mortar Platoon Leader, Rifle Platoon Leader, Rifle Company Executive Officer, and Headquarters Company Executive Officer in 1st Battalion (Mechanized), 31st Infantry Regiment, 2nd Infantry Division in the Republic of Korea.

Mr. Spiszer’s command positions include Company Commander of Company A, 3rd Battalion, 22nd Infantry Regiment, Schofield Barracks, Hawaii; Commander of the Polar Bears, 4th Battalion, 31st Infantry Regiment, Fort Drum, New York, with deployments to Afghanistan and Iraq; Commander of Task Force Duke, the 3rd Brigade Combat Team, 1st Infantry Division, Fort Hood, Texas, and Forward Operating Base Fenty, Afghanistan; and Commander of the Joint Multinational Readiness Center and Senior Commander of the Hohenfels Military Community, Hohenfels, Germany.

Other positions include Mortar Platoon Observer/Controller at the Joint Readiness Training Center, Fort Chaffee, Arkansas; Force Development and Modernization Officer for the 63rd Army Reserve Command, Los Alamitos, California; Assistant S-3 for the 3rd Battalion, 22nd Infantry and 1st Brigade, 25th Infantry Division, Schofield Barracks, Hawaii; Joint Staff Intern, J-5 Strategy Division and Action Officer in the Program Analysis and Evaluation Directorate of the Army Staff, at the Pentagon; Aide de Camp to the Deputy Commandant of the Command and General Staff College, Fort Leavenworth, Kansas; Chief of Plans for 1st Infantry Division, Wuerzburg, Germany and Camp Bondsteel, Kosovo; Battalion S-3 for 2nd Battalion, 2nd Infantry Regiment, Vilseck, Germany and Camp Monteith, Kosovo; Brigade Executive Officer for 3rd Brigade, 1st Infantry, Vilseck; Force Deployment Branch Chief, U.S. Army Europe, Heidelberg, Germany; Director of the Soldier Battle Lab at Fort Benning, Georgia; Executive Officer to the Commander of the International Security Assistance Force Joint Command, Kabul, Afghanistan; and Chief of Staff, V Corps and Deputy Chief of Staff, U.S. Army Europe, Wiesbaden, Germany.

As a DA Civilian, he has also served as the Director of the Army’s Security Assistance Training Field Activity at Fort Eustis, Virginia and as the Chief of Staff for U.S. Army Japan at Camp Zama, Japan.

Mr. Spiszer’s badges include the Army Staff, Joint Staff, Airborne, Combat and Expert Infantryman’s, and Combat Action Badges, the Ranger Tab, and the German Armed Forces Proficiency (in Gold), Leadership, and German and Austrian Sports Badges. His decorations include the Distinguished Service Medal, Legion of Merit with two oak leaf clusters, Bronze Star Medal with three oak leaf clusters, Purple Heart Medal, Meritorious Service Medal with six oak leaf clusters, the Meritorious Unit Award with two oak leaf clusters, the Army Superior Unit Award with two oak leaf clusters, the Department of State Meritorious Honor Award, and the Superior Civilian Service Award with oak leaf cluster and Japan’s Defense Cooperation Medal.

Mr. Spiszer is married to the former Melissa Kay Carter. They have two grown sons, John and Joseph.
