Colonel John 'Ron' Corson USA Ret. Biography

COL (Ret.) John “Ron” Corson served as an Infantry Officer for 28 Years followed by 18 Years experience in senior level management, operational test and evaluation and operations analysis in his civilian work as Senior Program manager, Vice President of a professional services government contractor, and President of JRC Research and Analysis, Inc. providing operational test and evaluation support to major Army and Joint Service systems and programs.

EDUCATION:
B.S. Industrial Management and Labor Relations, Drexel University, 1961
M.B.A. Logistics Management, Ohio State University, 1972 U
.S. Army War College, 1988

1998 - 2012 President, JRC RESEARCH & ANALYSIS, INC., Williamsburg, Virginia
President of a professional services company providing technical and analytical services to the US Army Operational Test and Evaluation Command (OPTEC) and DoD as a team member of VRC Corporation, Inc. Responsible for technical support of DoD and Army Projects in the area of planning, policy, and operational test and evaluation. Responsible for developing evaluation measures, technical support and evaluation services in support of the design and conduct of major weapons systems operational test and evaluations. Responsible primary support for the conduct of the OPTEC Basic Evaluator Couse conducted at government various OPTEC and TECOM sites in the USA.

1996 - 1997 Consultant, Williamsburg, Virginia
Private consultant under contract to VRC Corporation and United International Engineering, Inc. in support of DoD and Army Projects in the area of planning, policy, and operational test and evaluation. Developed successful baseline evaluation measures to assess the Army’s Advanced Warfighting Experiment (AWE) Warrior Focus 96. Evaluated the Theater Support Command (TSC) organizational concept during Prairie Warrior 96.

1994 - 1996 Chief Executive Officer, INTEGRATED VISUAL LEARNING, NEWPORT NEWS, VIRGINIA.
CEO of a joint venture that provides a unique multi-task remediation therapy for children with learning and reading disabilities. Responsible for the overall management and strategic development of the corporate joint-venture and for the conduct of therapy in a clinical setting for treatment of learning-disabled children. Responsible for the development of all management, marketing, financial, and training programs.

1989 - 1994 Vice President/Senior Program Manager, MANDEX, INC., NEWPORT NEWS, VIRGINIA.
Exercised senior program management responsibility for MANDEX's major Army training development and training support contract to the U.S. Army Training and Doctrine Command (TRADOC). Responsible for all technical and functional interfaces between TRADOC and MANDEX, and between MANDEX and its four TRADOC training support sub-contractors. Served in a strategic planning capacity for Army programs within MANDEX.

1987 - 1989 Deputy Commander, U.S. ARMY OPERATIONAL TEST ANDEVALUATION AGENCY (OTEA).
Responsible for daily management and operational priorities of over 300 military and civilian evaluators, engineers, operations research analysts and admin personnel. Controlled an operating budget and a research and development budget of over 70 million dollars. Developed Army operational test and evaluation policy. Orchestrated a new Army T&E planning and documentation process between OTEA and the U.S. Army TRADOC.

1985 - 1987 Chief, Combat Arms and Intelligence Systems Division, U.S. ARMY OPERATIONAL TEST AND EVALUATION AGENCY (OTEA).
Directed an evaluation division responsible for operational test and evaluation and Continuous Comprehensive Evaluation of all Army major combat arms and intelligence systems. Developed and directed the Combat Performance Operational Assessment of the Bradley vehicle and its High Survivability variants. Directed Army operational testing for the NAVSTAR Global Positioning System and user trials for the Army Intelligence All Source Analysis System.

1983 - 1985 Deputy Director, DIRECTORATE OF TRAINING & DOCTRINE, U.S. ARMY INFANTRY SCHOOL & Chief, INFANTRY PROPONENCY.
Coordinated the first comprehensive review of light Infantry forces for the Army Vice Chief of staff. Headed the L ight Infantry Task Force responsible for developing the unit training strategy and institutional training programs for the Army's new Light Infantry Divisions. Developed the simulation network system requirements for the Bradley Fighting Vehicle.

1980 - 1983 Battalion Commander, Mechanized Infantry Battalion, 3rd Battalion 36th Infantry, Kirschgoens, West Germany.
Commanded, trained, and led a combat ready 834-man mechanized infantry battalion Forward Deployed in West Germany. Planned and executed innovative General Defense Plan training. Awarded the U.S. Army Europe Outstanding Partnership Award for 1982.

1979 - 1980 Chief OR/SA and Logistics Analyst, USAREUR ORGANIZATIONAL AND OPERATIONAL ANALYSIS AGENCY.
Developed planning and analysis of logistics requirements for deployment of combat forces and relocation of support forces within Europe. Conducted a New Equipment Training Study for the 7th Army Training Command of resource, training and facilities requirements for the U.S. Army, Europe.

1978 - 1979 Executive Officer, Mechanized Infantry Battalion, 2nd Battalion 36th Infantry, Kirschgoens, West Germeny.
Responsible as second in command for training, maintenance, and readiness of a Mechanized Infantry Battalion in the 1st Brigade, 3rd Armor Division Forward Deployed in West Germany.

1977 - 1978 Deputy G-3, 2ND INFANTRY DIVISION, Camp Casey, Korea.
Responsible for supervision under the direction of the G-3 of the day to day operations, planning, and readiness of a US Army combat division stationed in Korea.

1973 - 1976 Chief, Cost Analysis Branch, SYSTEMS AND COST ANALYSIS DIVISION, U.S. ARMY LOGISTICS MANAGEMENT CENTER. Fort Lee, Virginia.
Developed parametric life cycle cost estimating procedures and executed economic analysis studies. Completed a highly effective economic analysis of automating the Army research and development budgeting process for the Army Deputy Chief of Staff for Research Development and Acquisition.

1970 - 1973 Student, Attended the US Army Infantry officer Advanced Course at Fort Benning, GA and MBA Program at The Ohio State University.

1969 - 1970 Light Infantry Company Commander and Brigade Operations Officer, U.S. ARMY, South Vietnam.
Commanded D Company 3/47 Infantry in the 2nd Brigade, Mobile Riverine Force of the 9th Infantry conducting combat operations if Vietnam Delta area. Assistant S-3 Task Force Carlson 6th Bn 31st US Infantry responsible for coordinating the defense of Dong Tam Operational Base. Commanded A Company 6/31 Infantry at Can Guoic, Vietnam. Operations Officer, 3rd Brigade, 9th Infantry Division, Tan An, Vietnam.

1965 - 1968 Industrial Engineer and Field Engineer, General Electric Company Louisville, Kentucky and Metuchen, New Jersey. Initially hired into a management trainee in the GE Manufacturing Training Program. Worked as a Manufacturing Engineer for the Room Air Conditioning manufacturing facility and then as a Foreman in the compressor manufacturing operation. Reassigned to a Marketing Trainee Program in the GE Air Conditioning Department. Worked as an Application Engineer for GE Commercial Air Conditioning products and instructor in the AC Department. Hired as a full-time GE employee as a f ield engineer for the Marketing Department of the GE Air Conditioning and Heating Products District in New York City or the northeast coast servicing Levitt & Sons Builders from NY to VA..

1965 - 1965 A Team Detachment Commander, 7th Special Forces Group, Fort Bragg, NC
After completing SFOC assigned as an “A” Detachment Commander 7th SFG. Responsible for training a 12-man Special Forces A Team focused for deployment to the European Theater of Operations.

1964 - 1965 Brigade Assistant S-3, 3rd Brigade, 3rd Infantry Division, Aschaffenburg, Germany
Assistant Operations Officer responsible for planning, training, and support of operations in a US Army Mechanized Infantry Brigade forward deployed in Germany as part in the NATO force defending Western Europe.

1962 - 1964 Platoon Leader and Assistant S-3 Air, 3rd Armored Rifle Battalion (ARB) Re-designated 2nd Battalion 15th Infantry, Wildflecken, Germany.
Served as a Mechanized Infantry Platoon Leader, Davy Crocket Section Leader, HHC Commander, and S-3 Air in a Mechanized Infantry Battalion attached to the 3rd Infantry Division Forward Deployed near the East German border as part of US Army forces in NATO.
